Cardiac Rehab

Cardiac rehab is a medically supervised program that includes exercise, support, and education.

Who needs to have cardiac rehab?

Cardiac rehab is important for people whoâ€™ve had some kind of problem with their heart, such as:

  • Heart Attack
  • Heart Failure
  • Use of a ventricular assist device.
  • Angina
  • Heart or heart-lung replacement
  • Heart valve repair or replacement
  • Coronary artery bypass graft.
  • Cornoary artery Angioplasty with or without stent placement

The national average of completion for cardiac rehabilitation programs is 7%. At the Rolling Plains Cardiac Rehab program, the average completion rate is over 50%.
